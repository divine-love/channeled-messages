When we are born we are set into the river of life which carries us to destinations and experiences which are hard to anticipate. Some of us go along unwilling to resist the current and often content to be carried. Others rail and flail as they fight this destiny with all their will. Most of us do a bit of both depending on our comfort level with what is happening at the time. Life is a powerful gift. It is precious beyond price. We cannot waste a drop of it yet we tend to abdicate from the obvious invitation to make something more of it outside of the cultural templates of the day. Doing so would involve scrutinizing ourselves and our lives on a deep level and if fault is found to act upon it. Taking this tact rather than following the path of least resistance involves accepting personal responsibility for all of our actions. In our youth we may focus energies on reform and change both in society and in our immediate environs. The fresh perspective offered by young adults is a gift to the established order. Society does not change quickly or easily. It requires deep commitment and energy. Too often these new perspectives are dismissed as youthful idealism, unfeasible and lacking in wisdom. Yet each generation brings a shift to the cultural norms and perspectives. Some shifts are profound while others more subtle or do not show up until years later.

We create our own truth and reality. Our thoughts and aspirations determine what direction we steer that ship of life. What one absorbs in our childhood and teen years reflect profoundly what the world looks like through our eyes. Many accept what is taught by our institutions as literal truth. We all push back to some degree and certainly question some norms, especially in our teen years. That's to be expected. How far things go from there is very telling of the vitality of a generation. How that generation uses the resources and knowledge available to them in order to enact reform determines a great deal in regards to change. How much the young are willing to dive down below the surface noise and determine what they really think is crucial to set a good foundation for change. The young have more power than they think and their choices as to how to use this power will either change the world irrevocably or keep the status quo.

Our planet earth is getting smaller for all of us. The use of social media, the availability of easy travel and the world wide dissemination of cultural information all contribute to this fact. For most of history we were neighbors in the country side requiring some effort to communicate and come together. Now we are living in a planetary apartment building acutely aware of every conversation and movement. The human world has evolved so rapidly in this regard and we are bombarded with so much information and stimulus that it is hard to know what are our own thoughts versus what we are absorbing from the ethers. Our spirit is being bullied about by powerful communication engines and the unending background noise of modern life. Streams of information are constant, keeping our thoughts floating on the surface of our awareness. With all of this distraction, it is difficult to really know what we think or feel as the presence outside stimulus is always with us. Like a screen filled with complex symbols with the noise level turned up high and full of static how can we get into deeper thought when our minds are so distracted? How many have the luxury of quiet contemplation and spiritual replenishment?

It is a challenging time for our world and everyone in it. Great poverty and inequities threaten to turn our lofty social structures upside down. The youth feel this inequity acutely as they ponder and struggle to make sense of their lives. All the while, the earth is suffering from a natural imbalance brought on by over population and consumerism on a vast scale. Humanity is on the brink of chaos if the rules of the game are not changed quickly and the healing of our home is not instituted ASAP. Those willing to contemplate such a future are faced with real challenges which are filled with the possibility of dire outcomes. Evolution can come slowly or quickly and is often determined by our environment and our own choices. Passivity allows the river to carry you along without your consent. Being armed with knowledge and a sound spiritual awareness can provide the strength needed to at least partially determine your course. Without self empowerment and the ability to see truth no matter how harsh that is, the outcomes of a life are not self determined but very much the result of outside forces. How we institute change within the wider world is very reliant on our personal choices and our ability to think without the clutter of a world gone a little crazy.

Thinking outside the box requires a certain amount of detachment from the outside world. Starting from that place of personal reform and moving forward from there is the key to making changes in the world. Banding together without this firm foundation is an invitation to unsound action. For those wanting to take on the world, they had better know their inner world and the outside world well and not be too concerned about being liked for their efforts. Reformers are mavericks, outsiders and often ostracized for their efforts. Violent reformers are given much harsher treatment. Those impatient with the slow progress of reform instituted by the power structures of this world are more prone to act irrationally and without much effect. Those determined souls who stick to their goals no matter what and do so from within the slow moving cogs of the structure are more likely to have success. A simple thing like voting, participating in your community and helping those in need around you can go a long way to changing this world. Rioting, expressing anger wherever you go and rejecting outright the world around you will lead to alienation and more anger. Choices are always present in whatever you do.

The dilemmas of life are not easy to solve. The push/pull of passivity and action are with us every day. Escaping and non action are tempting behaviors and easily attained but effective positive action for change is the option required to make a difference. If you ignore the leak in the ceiling, eventually there will be a flood and a much bigger problem. Hiding from the truth will have the same consequences. Facing up to who you are as a person is the first step and then seeing the world with the scales removed from your eyes is the next step towards reform. Taking the right action requires the acceptance of the truth about yourself and the world around you. The question is 'are you strong enough to pursue truth over all the half truths pedaled by governments and corporations?' These two elements control a good portion of our lives. They determine much of the information coming to us and it is hard to discern the truth from fiction.

Our youth have sharp minds, energy and a good education. If they are willing to focus these attributes towards change then the world should make room for them to contribute solutions to growing social and environmental issues. Their vitality and fresh perspective would add a much needed breath of fresh air to what is essentially an indulged and willfully blind social order. I believe that our saving grace lies in the gift of youthful zeal and desire for reform. Their challenge is to neutralize the fog of misinformation and informational clutter within them and around them in order to discern the truth. They also need to understand and acknowledge their spiritual centre. Wisdom does not come from the mind alone. It bubbles up from that deeper place called soul. Everyone has the potential to tap into that source of spiritual and intuitive knowing. Few get beyond the busy mind but for the strong and focused individual, there is a great treasure waiting. There are a variety of ways to get there. Some meditate while others stumble upon it through traumas and altered states of consciousness. God is present in that place of deep stillness but few are curious enough to go there. It takes a great deal of trying in order to break through the many barriers of conditioning and outside distractions. Yet without spiritual maturity, humanity is bound to repeat over and over its fundamentally destructive patterns. Can this generation break the chains of human ignorance and destructiveness? One can only hope that they see that something very fundamental and basic needs to change for our species to mature into what is our God given potential unrealized because we are too distracted to see the true goal of life. That is to love our brothers and sisters no matter who they are and to love this world in all its amazing forms and permutations. Life is a gift. Don't let it carry you down roads well travelled but lead to nowhere. Take the untried road which leads to reform and positive change as a tribute to this gift of life. Go with what you know is right. Seek truth in all things and above all be truthful to yourself. Good luck.

Al Fike
