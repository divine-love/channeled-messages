### 2016-04-12-af-keea-atta-kem | Talk on Receiving Guidance | 2016-04-12

- NOTE (witness, `divine-love-mediumship`) [batch-proposed, revised] : the angels "have an influence and bearing upon your thoughts... making a rapport with your minds and your souls", and that rapport continues past delivery: "We are with you as you read them and we are with all who read them and help to impress upon you the importance of any particular message", relevant to each particular life. Closest existing member 2016-03-27-af-seretta-kem (Foundation, how communication crosses from spirit to mortal); nearest in spirit 2019-01-26-af-andrew (Elaboration, widening the capacity from designated mediums to everyone). Held at witness level rather than the Elaboration originally proposed: the claim is stated in two sentences inside a broader talk and does not develop the thread's mechanism or discernment strands.
    
- NOTE (possible holding-pen candidate, the recorded message as a living channel) [batch-proposed] : distinct from `divine-love-mediumship`, which covers how communication crosses to the medium and what makes it trustworthy. The claim here is about what happens to a message _after_ it is delivered: the recorded text continues to carry angelic accompaniment, and the angels attend each reader individually, impressing which message matters and how it applies to that life. "All of it is a training, an education, a direction to your lives." The argument it could trace, if a second witness ever develops it: guidance reaches the soul by rapport -> channelled messages are one form of that guidance -> the rapport does not end at delivery but attends every later reader -> therefore the written record is itself an instrument, not a transcript.
    
    - Sightings: 2016-04-12-af-keea-atta-kem (the conduit claim) and 2016-04-12-af-moses (the boundary on it, added at curator direction).
    - **2016-04-12-af-moses, proposed role [Objection-removed]** : clears the belief that would otherwise distort the conduit claim into bibliolatry. Moses names the error from the inside ("We, the Jews, placed great import upon the written word. We felt that the written word was the Word of God"), denies it ("For the word is not sacred. No, words are words"), relocates the sacredness ("What is sacred is your belief and connection, your rapport with your Heavenly Father"), and then preserves the conduit rather than discarding it: "to bring a suggestion of Truth in the written word, for that is merely all that it is, is a productive action." That last clause is what makes it a member of this candidate rather than only of `religious-error`: it keeps the written message useful while stripping it of authority.
        - Closest existing member: none, this candidate has no minted members. Nearest neighbour in the archive is the `religious-error` thread, whose argument it also serves; the two readings are compatible and the message can carry both.
        - New facet against Keea: Keea establishes that the recorded message keeps working on the reader; Moses sets the limit, that this is true of the message as conduit and never of the words as sacred object.
        - Alternative role if you prefer: Reframe. Objection-removed chosen because the belief in the sacred written word actively blocks the next step of the argument.
    - **Minting threshold now met, awaiting your direction.** The holding pen's standing rule is to mint once a second message gives the candidate an argument to trace. With Moses added there are two messages and a traceable argument. I have not minted anything. If you want it minted, it needs a slug; `message-as-living-channel` or `conduit-not-scripture` both read accurately, the second being closer to what the pair actually argues.
    - Worth weighing at build time: both messages were given to the same circle on the same evening, and both turn on the same word. Keea has the angels "making a rapport with your minds and your souls"; Moses has "your rapport with your Heavenly Father" as the thing that is actually sacred. The pairing looks deliberate rather than coincidental.
    - Search note: no third witness anywhere in the 497 messages currently indexed in llms.txt. That set is roughly a quarter of the archive, so the unprocessed 2016 to 2026 material may well supply more.
